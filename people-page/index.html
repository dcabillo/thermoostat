Resourcing

The goal of this module is to help other campuses find the human and material resources needed to run a Participatory Thermal Sensing (PTS) program.

Learn more

TherMOOstat, or a thermal participatory sensing program, needs a diverse group of individuals to keep the program running and maximize the use of incoming data. In this module, we hope to show you the key roles on our team and how to look for similar skills and/or knowledge in your teams. Although there are several people working with TherMOOstat, they all work part-time on the project, and in sum their time amounts to the equivalent of one full-time employee.

In this module, we also hope to describe how to find these resources and/or team members on your campus. Our team's success is rooted in our position within the Facilities department, under the UC Davis Energy Manager. We believe this is the best position to affect change with comfort feedback, but other possibilities or partnerships include a sustainability office or student housing group.

Major Roles

These roles are needed for the everyday operation of TherMOOstat. They include responsibilities such as reading and responding to incoming comfort votes, tracking and summarizing monthly votes and using the votes to inform future energy projects on campus.

Triage and Short-Term Feedback Tracking

John is a full-time project manager, who spends roughly 25% of his time on TherMOOstat related work.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Triage comfort feedback in real-time
  • Weekly reviews to check room temperatures and set points
  • Collaboration with HVAC techs in Facilities when issues are found

    • John has been working on the UC Davis campus for over 15 years. His knowledge of campus buildings is extensive, and there's history behind every building. John has a background in project management, with experience in HVAC systems and building automation systems.

    Engineers

    The Energy & Engineering Office has a group of 3-4 engineers that are brought into projects as needed. These engineers help consult on potential energy savings when TherMOOstat projects are conducted on campus.

    For example, energy and HVAC controls engineers are heavily involved in a project to create dynamic temperature set points on campus to avoid overcooling and overheating spaces on campus.

    TherMOOstat's role for the engineering team is to provide comfort data for energy efficiency projects on campus.

    Social Scientists

    TherMOOstat is an ideal platform for research because of it's survey format and the ability to get responses from a large group of people on campus.

    Research on thermal satisfaction in indoor spaces on campus and the community's willingness to vote for changes in thermostat settings in order to conserve energy are two areas of research made possible by TherMOOstat data.

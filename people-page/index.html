Resourcing

A successful Participatory Thermal Sensing (PTS) program requires a team with diverse skills to promote the program and maximize the utility of incoming data. The goal of this module is to help you identify the human resources you will need. We begin by describing what it takes to launch a PTS program. We then consider key responsibilities and examples of specific staff roles for ongoing program operations in terms of three main program goals: branding and engagement, analysis and triage, and applications. Finally, job descriptions are provided that you can post to find student intern support.

Prepare for Program Launch

To get a PTS program started you will likely need someone or several people who can:

  • Gain support from Facilities, e.g., convincing them of program benefits and assuring them it will not create too much more work for their technicians
  • [If launching in a lab, office or other non-classroom]: An ambassador, such as a well-known department member or the building manager, to share the PTS tool with selected building(s)
  • Create or inform, and gain necessary approvals for, campus-specific program branding if desired, such as program name, mascot, and messaging

Branding & Engagement Resources

Ongoing promotion of your PTS program is crucial to inform and engage potential users, and to sustain rates of feedback submissions. So you will want someone consistent on your team to lead these efforts, whether it is someone within the Facilities energy management team or perhaps someone in the campus sustainability office.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Create promotional items with program branding to display around campus
  • Attend campus events (e.g., student or new employee orientations) to promote, and/or offer content to other groups to promote the PTS program
  • Reach out to professors on campus to seek student interns and/or provide them with PTS data for use in course projects
  • Send emails to users with updates or information about the HVAC system related to their feedback (e.g., if they leave comments)

Key Requirements:

  • Knowledge of how the PTS system works and key messaging is essential for creating branding, promotional materials, and other communications
  • Graphic design skills and/or a communications background are a big help for designing promotional campaigns

Triage Resources

Triage and analysis should be performed by someone knowledgeable about campus energy systems and well-connected with campus HVAC technicians, in order to understand the causes of comfort issues, identify energy inefficiencies, and communicate to get problems fixed. Some communication with PTS users is also important in triage and analysis. Giving users direct and specific information about their reported concerns or significant discomfort communicates your respect for their well-being and also makes for more informed building occupants, building energy awareness on campus.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Addressing/fixing malfunctioning equipment in the BAS
  • Read incoming feedback and analyze the mechanical system of corresponding space, making notes for later reference (e.g., for other team members)
  • Weekly reviews to check room temperatures and set points for rooms where feedback was submitted

Key Requirements:

  • Technical knowledge of HVAC systems on campus
  • Access to Building Management System(s)
  • Access to building floor plans and mechanical drawings
  • Access to the campus traditional work order system
  • Collaborative relationships with HVAC techs in Facilities

Applications Resources

Your PTS program will gather a wealth of data, with many possible applications. In addition to triaging incoming data, you will also want team members paying attention to the bigger picture, which includes digging deeper to understand issues, collecting more data, and making decisions about energy efficiency projects based on insights from longer-term data analysis. The human resources required for this aspect include three main roles: Long-term data analysis; site visits; and project management.

Long-term Data Analysis: Key Responsibilities and Requirements

  • Analyze PTS data on a quarterly and annual basis. Lots of patterns will only emerge with aggregate, accumulated data. This should amount to about 1 day of work for one person per month on average.
  • Analysis should involve triangulation with other data sets, such as room temperature, building metadata, and outside air temperature, so access to those additional data sets is required, as are data handling skills, including file-merging.
  • If these analyses are performed by someone other than the person who conducts short-term analysis (a student intern does much of the long-term data analysis at UCD), there should be close collaboration between the two.

Project Management: Key Responsibilities and Requirements

  • Someone who has experience with project scoping and implementation is required to fully leverage PTS data for major projects, e.g., ceiling fan installation to address hot complaints
  • Collaboration with engineers and project managers
  • This role is not continuous, but as needed. At UC Davis we use at least a year of data to inform a new energy project, and to inform current projects we look at PTS data during and directly following implementation.
  • At UC Davis this role is performed by a project manager, but could also be performed by an engineer if they have projects out on campus and need PTS data.

Hiring Student Interns

Student interns are a great way to supplement your team’s existing human resources for a PTS program. They can help with promotions or assist in analyzing and responding to PTS comfort votes. Look for certain majors based on the tasks you need done. For example, engineering, economics and/or statistics students do well in data analysis roles, while communications, design, and english majors do well in communication roles; environmental science students are perhaps most versatile.

Key Responsibilities and Requirements:

  • Conduct site visits
  • Install data loggers
  • Ability to place and use a temperature data logger, and make observations
  • Knowledge of a room’s mechanical system is helpful but not necessary
  • Knowledge of the PTS system and key messages

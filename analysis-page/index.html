Questions to Consider

Here are some questions for your PTS team to consider as you make your plan of attack for dealing with the data:

  • Level of Control

    How much control do campus building occupants have over thermal conditions (control of thermostats, windows, etc.)?

    How much remote visibility do you have into room temperatures on campus?

    Are you able to make changes to a building automation system if necessary?

  • Taking Action

    When you can control the BAS, when will you make changes based on PTS feedback? When will you not?

    Will you ever submit a formal work request based on PTS feedback? Under what conditions?

  • Thresholds for Action

    What conditions do you consider the HVAC system to be functioning?

    What is the acceptable indoor temperature range on your campus?

Understanding Users

Understanding your users is a big help when it comes to deciding how to respond to their feedback. At UC Davis, we’ve created a matrix that describes different kinds of TherMOOstat users based on experience and research with the tool over the past 4+ years. We characterize users by their expectations and how much they move around the campus.
We suggest actions during data triage based on where a user lies in the matrix. A diagram like this helps us decide what actions we will take on incoming feedback, and also helps those new to the PTS understand how to react and respond to comfort feedback. Click the link below to see the matrix.
